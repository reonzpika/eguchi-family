# Business Frameworks & Validation Phase - Design Document

## Overview
The bridge between "I have an idea" and "I'm building a project". This phase helps family members validate their business idea through natural conversation, then structures it into an actionable project - all while maintaining momentum and excitement.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Speed over perfection** - Get them building, not overthinking
2. **AI as partner** - Not teacher or coach, but collaborative thinking partner  
3. **Always supportive** - Suggest scaled-down versions, never discourage
4. **Invisible frameworks** - Use business model thinking without the jargon
5. **No analysis paralysis** - Fast validation, quick decisions

---

## The Complete Flow: Idea → Project

### **STAGE 1: Idea Exploration Chat**
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes  
**Tone:** Excited brainstorming with a supportive partner  
**Goal:** Understand the idea and validate basic viability

#### AI Partner Questions (Adaptive)
AI asks these conversationally, not as a checklist. Adapts based on answers.

**Core Questions:**
1. "Who needs this? Can you describe them?"
   - *Validates: Target market exists*
   
2. "What problem does this solve for them?"
   - *Validates: Clear value proposition*
   
3. "How are they solving it now?"
   - *Validates: Understanding of competition/alternatives*
   
4. "What's the absolute simplest version you could start with?"
   - *Validates: Realistic scope*
   
5. "What would you charge? What feels right to you?"
   - *Validates: Pricing intuition*
   
6. "What would it cost you to deliver one?" (materials, time, tools)
   - *Validates: Unit economics*
   
7. "How would people find you?"
   - *Validates: Customer acquisition strategy*

**Additional probes (as needed):**
- "Have you seen anyone else doing something similar?"
- "What makes your version different or better?"
- "Do you already have the skills/tools to do this?"
- "How much time would this take per week?"
- "What's the first thing you'd need to do to start?"

#### AI Red Flag Detection

**While conversing, AI detects:**

| Red Flag | AI Response Strategy |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Resource mismatch** (wants physical store, has no capital) | Suggest digital/home-based version |
| **Time mismatch** (complex project, only 2 hours/week) | Suggest simpler scope or outsourcing |
| **Skill gap** (wants to code app, never coded) | Suggest no-code tools or learning path first |
| **Saturated market + no differentiation** | Ask: "What would make yours special?" |
| **Costs > Revenue** | Rework pricing or reduce costs |
| **No clear customer** | Help narrow target audience |

**Example Responses:**
- ❌ "This won't work" 
- ✅ "A physical bakery needs significant investment. What if you started by selling your signature cookies at weekend markets? You could test the idea with almost no risk."
- ✅ "Building an app from scratch takes months. What if you started with an Instagram account teaching your techniques, then created a course later?"

### **STAGE 2: AI Business Summary Generation**

After the conversation, AI synthesizes everything into a one-page summary.

#### Summary Structure

```markdown
# [Business Idea Name]

## 🎯 The Idea
[One sentence pitch]

## 👥 Who It's For  
[Target customer description - specific, not generic]

## 💡 The Problem You Solve
[What pain/need this addresses]

## 🛠️ Your Solution
[How you'll solve it - simplified version]

## ✨ How You're Different
[Your unique angle/advantage]

## 💰 Money Math
**Simple Economics:**
- Sell for: ¥X per [unit]
- Costs: ¥Y per [unit]  
- Profit: ¥Z per [unit]

**Realistic first month:**
- If you sell [N] units: ¥[revenue]
- After costs: ¥[profit]

## 🎣 First Customers
[Where/how to find initial customers]

## ✅ Reality Check

Based on your profile:
- ✅ Matches your available time (5-10 hours/week)
- ✅ Uses your existing skills (cooking, social media)
- ⚠️ Competitive market - need strong differentiation
- ✅ Low startup cost (under ¥50,000)
- ✅ Can start from home

## 🚀 Immediate Next Step
[One specific action they can take this week]
```

#### Tone Guidelines for Summary
- Encouraging but honest
- Specific numbers (not vague)
- Celebrates strengths, acknowledges challenges
- Makes it feel doable
- Creates excitement about starting

### **STAGE 3: The Decision Point**

**AI asks:** "This could work! Want to turn it into a project and start building?"

**User options:**

#### Option A: "Yes, let's do it!" 
→ Proceeds to Project Creation (Stage 4)

#### Option B: "Let me think about it"
→ Idea saved for later
- Can upgrade to project anytime
- AI might check in after a week: "Still thinking about [idea]?"
- No pressure, no judgment

#### Option C: "Can we explore a different idea?"
→ Returns to idea generation from their profile

### **STAGE 4: Project Creation** (Auto-magic)

When user says yes to creating a project, AI automatically:

#### 4A: Framework Selection (Invisible)

AI identifies business type and selects appropriate framework:

| Business Type | Framework Used | Key Focus |
|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Service-based** (consulting, coaching, teaching) | Service Blueprint | Customer journey + delivery process |
| **Physical Product** (crafts, food, goods) | Lean Canvas | Problem, solution, channels, costs |
| **Digital Product** (courses, templates, apps) | Value Proposition Canvas | Customer pains & gains |
| **Marketplace/Platform** | Two-Sided Market Canvas | Both customer segments |
| **Content/Creator** (YouTube, blog) | Creator Business Model | Audience → monetization |

**User never sees framework names** - they just get good questions and structure.

#### 4B: Living Document Creation

AI generates initial living document with framework-appropriate sections.

**Example: Physical Product (Lean Canvas-based)**

```markdown
# [Project Title]

## 🌟 Vision
[What success looks like - based on their "success vision" from profile]

## 🎯 Problem
[The specific problem being solved]

## 👥 Customer Segments
[Who experiences this problem]

## ✨ Unique Value Proposition  
[Why this solution is compelling]

## 💡 Solution
[How the product solves the problem - simplified version]

## 📣 Channels
[How customers will discover and buy]

## 💰 Revenue Streams
[How money is made]

## 💸 Cost Structure
[What it costs to deliver]

## 🏆 Unfair Advantage
[What makes this hard to copy - their unique skills/position]

## 📊 Key Metrics
[How to measure success - start simple]
- Units sold per month
- Customer feedback
- Profit margin

## 🚀 Current Status
**Phase:** Planning  
**Started:** [Date]  
**Last Updated:** [Date]

## 📋 Next Steps
[Generated milestones - see section 4C]
```

**Example: Service-based (Service Blueprint-based)**

```markdown

## 🌟 Vision  
[Their desired outcome]

## 👥 Who I Help
[Target client description]

## 💡 Service Offering

**Phase:** Planning
```

#### 4C: Milestone Generation

AI creates **3 initial milestones** tailored to user's profile.

**Calibration based on profile:**
- **Low confidence (1-2) + Limited time (2-5h/week):**
  - Very small, quick-win milestones
  - 1-2 week timeframes
  - Low-risk actions
  
- **Medium confidence (3) + Moderate time (5-10h/week):**
  - Balanced milestones
  - 2-4 week timeframes
  - Mix of planning and action
  
- **High confidence (4-5) + Abundant time (10+h/week):**
  - Ambitious milestones
  - 2-6 week timeframes
  - Action-heavy

**Example Milestones (Low confidence, limited time):**

```markdown
## Milestone 1: Validate the Idea (Week 1-2)
**Goal:** Confirm people actually want this

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Talk to 3 potential customers (friends/family OK!)
- [ ] Ask: "Would you pay ¥X for this?"
- [ ] Document their feedback

**Success:** At least 2 people say "yes, I'd buy that"

---

## Milestone 2: Create Minimum Viable Offering (Week 3-4)
**Goal:** Make the simplest version possible

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Create basic version (don't aim for perfect!)
- [ ] Take simple photos/description
- [ ] Set up way to accept orders (even just messaging)

**Success:** Someone could buy from you if they wanted to

---

## Milestone 3: Get First Customer (Week 5-6)
**Goal:** Make your first sale

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Share with 10 people (social media, messaging)
- [ ] Offer special "founding customer" discount
- [ ] Deliver your first order

**Success:** At least one paying customer + feedback
```

**Example Milestones (High confidence, abundant time):**

```markdown
## Milestone 1: Market Research & Brand (Week 1-2)
**Goal:** Understand market and establish presence

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Research 5 competitors - what do they charge? What's missing?
- [ ] Create basic brand identity (name, logo, colors)
- [ ] Set up Instagram + simple website
- [ ] Interview 5 target customers about their needs

**Success:** Clear brand identity + deep customer understanding

---

## Milestone 2: Product Development (Week 3-5)
**Goal:** Create professional offering

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Develop first product line (3-5 items)
- [ ] Professional product photography
- [ ] Price based on costs + market research
- [ ] Create sample products

**Success:** Portfolio of products ready to sell

---

## Milestone 3: Launch Campaign (Week 6-8)
**Goal:** Public launch with sales target

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Pre-launch buzz (teasers, countdown)
- [ ] Launch event (online or in-person)
- [ ] Run promotional campaign
- [ ] Process minimum 10 orders

**Success:** 10+ sales in first month + customer testimonials
```

#### 4D: Immediate Action Suggestion

AI always ends with: **"This week, here's what to focus on:"**

One specific, achievable task from Milestone 1:
- "Talk to 3 friends about your idea and ask if they'd buy it"
- "Spend 30 minutes sketching out your first product design"
- "Create an Instagram account and post your first idea"

**Criteria for immediate action:**
- Doable in under 2 hours
- Requires no money
- Moves them forward
- Builds momentum

## Framework Details (AI Internal Use)

### Lean Canvas (Physical Products)
**Focus:** Problem → Solution → Business Model

**Key Questions AI Asks:**
1. Problem: What problem exists?
2. Customer Segments: Who has this problem?
3. Unique Value Prop: Why you vs alternatives?
4. Solution: What are you making?
5. Channels: How will they find/buy it?
6. Revenue Streams: How do you make money?
7. Cost Structure: What does it cost?
8. Key Metrics: How do you measure success?
9. Unfair Advantage: What's hard to copy?

### Service Blueprint (Service Businesses)
**Focus:** Customer Experience + Backend Delivery

**Key Questions AI Asks:**
1. Customer Actions: What does client do?
2. Frontstage (Visible): What do they experience?
3. Backstage (Invisible): What happens behind scenes?
4. Support Processes: What enables delivery?
5. Physical Evidence: What do they see/receive?

### Value Proposition Canvas (Digital Products)
**Focus:** Deep customer understanding

**Key Questions AI Asks:**
1. Customer Jobs: What are they trying to do?
2. Pains: What frustrates them?
3. Gains: What would delight them?
4. Pain Relievers: How do you reduce pains?
5. Gain Creators: How do you create gains?
6. Products & Services: What you offer

### Two-Sided Market Canvas (Platforms)
**Focus:** Both sides of marketplace

**Key Questions AI Asks:**
1. Supply Side: Who provides?
2. Demand Side: Who consumes?
3. Value to Supply: Why would providers join?
4. Value to Demand: Why would consumers use?
5. Network Effects: How does it get better with more users?

### Creator Business Model (Content)
**Focus:** Audience → Multiple Revenue Streams

**Key Questions AI Asks:**
1. Content Type: What do you create?
2. Audience: Who watches/reads?
3. Distribution: Where do they find you?
4. Monetization: How do you earn? (ads, sponsors, products, memberships)
5. Growth: How do you get more viewers?

## Validation Components

### What We're Validating

#### Market Validation
- ✅ Target audience is specific enough
- ✅ Problem is painful enough they'd pay
- ✅ Market size is sufficient (even locally)

#### Economic Validation  
- ✅ Unit economics work (revenue > costs)
- ✅ Pricing is realistic for market
- ✅ Profit margins are viable

#### Feasibility Validation
- ✅ Matches user's available time
- ✅ Uses existing skills (or has learning path)
- ✅ Startup costs are within reach
- ✅ Can be done from their situation (location, space, etc.)

#### Competitive Validation
- ✅ Competition exists but not overwhelming
- ✅ Clear differentiation point
- ✅ "Unfair advantage" identified

### What We're NOT Validating (Yet)

❌ Perfect product-market fit (learn by doing)  
❌ Detailed financial projections (too early)  
❌ Comprehensive market research (analysis paralysis)  
❌ Legal/regulatory deep dive (address when needed)  
❌ Scalability plans (focus on first customers first)

## AI Behavior Guidelines

### Always Do:
✅ Celebrate their idea first ("That's interesting!")  
✅ Ask "why" to understand motivation  
✅ Suggest scaled-down versions if too ambitious  
✅ Use their actual words back to them  
✅ Reference their profile (skills, time, confidence)  
✅ Give specific examples from NZ context  
✅ End with clear next action

### Never Do:
❌ Use business jargon without explanation  
❌ Discourage or say "that won't work"  
❌ Ask about things they can't know yet  
❌ Overwhelm with too many questions at once  
❌ Compare them to successful businesses  
❌ Create fear about competition  
❌ Make them feel inadequate

### Tone Examples:

**Good:**
- "What if you started by selling just your signature cookies at weekend markets? Way less risk than opening a shop."
- "I notice you mentioned limited time - how about we focus on products you can batch-make on weekends?"
- "That's a crowded space, but your background in X gives you a unique angle. Let's play that up."

**Bad:**
- "The bakery market is saturated. You'll need significant capital for a lean startup model."
- "Have you done a competitive analysis and identified your unique value proposition?"
- "Most food businesses fail in the first year. Are you sure about this?"

## Technical Implementation Notes

### AI Model Requirements
- Context retention across multi-turn conversation
- Pattern matching for red flags
- Framework selection logic
- Template generation from conversation
- Milestone difficulty calibration
- Natural language generation (summary, living doc)

### Data Flow
```
1. User starts idea exploration
   ↓
2. AI conducts adaptive conversation
   ↓
3. AI stores conversation insights
   ↓
4. AI generates business summary
   ↓
5. User decides: Project or not
   ↓
6. If project:
   - Select framework
   - Generate living doc
   - Create milestones  
   - Store in database
   - Show to user
```

## Edge Cases & Special Situations

### User Has Multiple Ideas
- Save all as separate ideas
- Help them pick one to focus on first
- "You have 3 great ideas! Which one excites you most to start with?"

### User Wants to Pivot Mid-Conversation
- "Actually, I changed my mind about..."
- AI adapts: "No problem! Let's explore this new direction."
- Updates conversation context

### User Gets Discouraged During Validation
- "This seems harder than I thought..."
- AI reframes: "Every business has challenges. Want to adjust the scope to make it easier?"
- Offers simpler version

### User Bypasses Validation, Wants to Start Immediately
- Respect their decision
- Create project with lighter structure
- AI: "Great energy! I've set up your project. As you go, I'll help you think through [validation topics]."
